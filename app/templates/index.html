Paul Wallington was born in Johannesburg in 1993 and raised in Plettenberg Bay. He is in his final year at the Michaelis school of fine art Cape Town, South Africa. Paul works predominantly in oil painting where he draws strong inspiration from South African literature and poetry. His paintings exist within a larger narrative set by writers who’s stories hold a powerful resonance within South Africa today. These novels attempt to explain or dissect the ongoing narrative within South Africa and challenge our idea of truth. That “truth” is further muddied when created on the canvas through oil paint. It is in this world of visual metaphors where these ideas become tangible. Strong links to artists such as Francis Bacon and Francisco Goya are visible in his paintings.
